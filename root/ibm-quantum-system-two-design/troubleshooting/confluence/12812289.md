This document outlines common issues, diagnostic steps, and resolutions when working with IBM Quantum systems and the Qiskit SDK.

### Backend Not Found

-   **Symptoms:** `QiskitBackendNotFoundError`.
    
-   **Diagnosis:** The hardware backend you are trying to access has been retired, is offline for maintenance, or your account tier does not have access permissions for that specific system.
    
-   **Resolution:** 1. Check your available backends using `service.backends()`.
    
    2\. Verify the operational status of the system on the IBM Quantum Platform dashboard.
    
    3\. Switch to an available system or use a simulator (e.g., `ibmq_qasm_simulator`).

## 3\. Job Submission & Execution

Understanding the IBM Quantum queuing system is critical for managing job expectations.

### Job Stuck in Queue

-   **Symptoms:** Job status remains `QUEUED` for an extended period (hours or days).
    
-   **Diagnosis:** IBM Quantum systems use a fair-share scheduling algorithm. Your job priority may be low if you have submitted many recent jobs. Alternatively, the system may be offline for calibration.

    3.  If the system is offline for maintenance, leave the job in the queue; it will process automatically when the system comes back online. Do not cancel and resubmit, as you will lose your place in line.
        

### Job Failed

-   **Symptoms:** Job status returns `ERROR` or `FAILED`.
    
-   **Diagnosis:** This can occur due to circuit timeouts, exceeding the maximum number of shots, or exceeding the maximum circuit depth for the selected backend.
    
-   **Resolution:**
    
    1.  Fetch the error message using `job.error_message()`.
        
    2.  Verify that your `shots` parameter does not exceed the backend's limit (often 20,000 or 100,000 depending on the system).
        
    3.  Ensure your circuit depth does not exceed the limits of the target hardware's coherence time.
